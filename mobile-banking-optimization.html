UX & Business Analysis Case Study

Mobile Banking App: Streamlining the Fund Transfer Flow

A comprehensive end-to-end UX analysis diagnosing a 68% task abandonment rate in P2P mobile transfers. Covers problem definition, heuristic evaluation, flow redesign, system requirements, and projected KPIs.

1
Define the usability crisis
Phase 1: Discovery

Despite a large user base, the mobile banking application suffers from a severe friction point: users are failing to complete simple peer-to-peer (P2P) fund transfers. Qualitative app store reviews indicate frustration with:

Buried navigation menus Clunky 2FA/Passwords Repetitive manual data entry Anxiety-inducing lag Vague error states
"I just want to send $50 to my brother. Why do I have to open a side menu, find 'Transfers', type his account number again, and wait 3 minutes for an SMS code? It's exhausting." — App Store Review

3
Heuristic evaluation
Phase 3: Root Causes
Issue

High Interaction Cost

Key actions (Transfers, Pay Bills) require 3+ taps just to locate within a nested hamburger menu.

Issue

Authentication Friction

Users must type complex alphanumeric passwords on mobile keyboards, leading to high error rates.

Issue

Lack of Smart Defaults

Users transfer money to the same 2-3 people frequently, yet must enter account/routing numbers manually every time.

Issue

Poor System Feedback

No visual indication (spinners/skeletons) that the system is processing, causing users to tap 'Submit' twice and trigger errors.

4
Proposed UX Solutions
Phase 4: Ideation
Solution

Dashboard Action Widgets

Bring high-frequency tasks ("Quick Transfer") directly to the home screen, bypassing the menu entirely.

Solution

Biometric First

Default to FaceID/Fingerprint login to reduce login time from ~12 seconds to ~1.5 seconds.

Solution

"Recent Payees" Carousel

Surface avatars of the 4 most recently paid individuals for 1-tap pre-filling of account details.

Solution

Optimistic UI & Skeletons

Use skeleton loading screens and disable the submit button immediately upon tap to prevent duplicate calls.

Functional & Non-Functional Requirements
Phase 7: Engineering Handoff
  • FR
    REQ-01 Quick Widget: The system shall display a "Quick Transfer" widget on the main dashboard immediately upon authentication.
  • FR
    REQ-02 Smart Caching: The system shall store and surface the 4 most recent successful transfer recipients for 1-tap selection.
  • FR
    REQ-03 Biometric Auth: The system shall utilize native device APIs (FaceID/TouchID/Android Biometrics) to authorize transfers, replacing SMS OTPs where legally compliant.
  • NFR
    REQ-04 Optimistic UI: Front-end state must update instantly upon action, displaying a skeleton loader to prevent duplicate API calls while the backend processes the transaction.

Interview Summary (STAR Method)
S

Situation: Our mobile banking app had a high drop-off rate (68%) during peer-to-peer money transfers, leading to poor App Store ratings and increased customer support calls.

T

Task: I needed to identify the friction points in the user journey and design a solution that increased the transfer completion rate without compromising financial security.

A

Action: I conducted a funnel analysis that revealed severe drops at the "Menu Navigation" and "Manual Entry" stages. I redesigned the architecture by moving "Quick Transfers" directly to the dashboard, implementing "Recent Payee" avatars for 1-tap data entry, and replacing slow SMS OTPs with native biometric confirmation (FaceID). I drafted strict accessibility (WCAG) and functional requirements for engineering handoff.

R

Result: The redesign collapsed the flow from 9 steps down to 5, projecting a 42% increase in task completion, a 55-second reduction in time-to-transfer, and a vastly improved user satisfaction score.
