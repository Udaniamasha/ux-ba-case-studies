3
Identify usability issues
Step 3 of 8
Issue

Complex forms

Unnecessary fields bloat the form and increase cognitive load.

Issue

No guest checkout

Mandatory account creation is the #1 cited reason for checkout abandonment globally.

Issue

No progress indicator

Users don't know how many steps remain, increasing anxiety and drop-off probability.

Issue

Poor error handling

Errors shown only after submission, not inline. Users must scroll to find which field failed.

Issue

Confusing navigation

Back button loses form data. No clear way to edit a previous step without restarting.

Issue

Hidden total cost

Shipping and tax revealed only at payment stage — classic sticker shock abandonment trigger.

4
Propose solutions
Step 4 of 8
Fix

Guest checkout first

Lead with email only. Offer account creation post-purchase as an upsell, not a gate.

Fix

Collapse to 2 steps

Merge shipping + payment into one page. Combine address and shipping method selection.

Fix

Add progress indicator

A persistent step bar shows current position and remaining steps, reducing uncertainty.

Fix

Inline validation

Validate each field on blur. Show green tick for success, red inline message for errors.

Fix

Cost upfront

Show estimated shipping in the cart. Persistent order summary visible throughout checkout.

Fix

Multiple payments

Add Apple Pay, Google Pay, BNPL as express options above the form.

7
Functional requirements
Step 7 of 8
  • FR
    System shall allow guest checkout — users must not be required to create an account to complete a purchase.
  • FR
    System shall validate user input in real-time — each field is validated on blur with inline success or error feedback.
  • FR
    System shall display total cost before payment — subtotal, shipping, and tax visible throughout checkout, not only at payment.
  • FR
    System shall support multiple payment methods — credit/debit card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and at least one BNPL provider.
  • FR
    System shall display a progress indicator — a persistent step bar shows the current step and remaining steps at all times.
  • FR
    System shall support address auto-complete — integration with a postcode/address lookup API to reduce manual entry.
  • FR
    System shall confirm order with a summary page — confirmation must include order number, item list, delivery address, and estimated arrival.
  • FR
    System shall allow editing without data loss — users can return to a previous section and modify entries without restarting checkout.

Interview gold — "How did you reduce drop-offs?" (STAR answer)
S

Situation: The platform had a 73% cart abandonment rate — only 27 in 100 users who added items ever completed a purchase.

T

Task: Identify root causes through funnel analysis and propose a redesigned checkout that removes friction at each drop-off point.

A

Action: I mapped the 5-step funnel and found the biggest leaks — 31% dropped at "Enter Details" due to forced login and form complexity, and 17% at Payment after seeing shipping costs for the first time. I redesigned the flow from 6 steps to 3, introduced guest-first checkout, combined address and payment into one screen, surfaced cost estimates from the cart onward, and added express pay. I documented findings as functional requirements and low-fidelity wireframes for the engineering team.

R

Result: Projected a 31% increase in completion rate, a 38% reduction in abandonment, and a 42-second drop in average checkout time. The guest checkout change alone addressed the single largest drop-off point.
